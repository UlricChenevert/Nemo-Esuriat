Feeds 4 humans and one dog comfortably. Best ribs ever.

The Dry Rub

1 tbsp Paprika
1 tbsp Garlic powder
1 tbsp Onion powder
1 tsp Cayenne (adjust based on heat preference)
2 tsp Salt & 1 tsp Black pepper

The Braising Liquid

2 cans/bottles Budweiser
2 tbsp Dijon mustard
3 tbsp Dill pickle sauce
2 tbsp Sriracha

The Finishing Glaze

1.5 cups Barbecue sauce
1 tbsp Sriracha (optional, for extra heat)

Instructions

1. Mix together dry rub in medium cooking bowl
2. Coat the ribs generously on both sides
3. Cover ribs in foil / plastic wrap (or in a big enough container) and leave ribs in oven overnight

4. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C).
5. Mix braising liquid together
6. Lay out two large sheets of heavy-duty aluminum foil. Place the ribs on the foil and fold up the edges to create a "boat."
7. Carefully pour the liquid into the foil boat around the ribs (try not to wash the rub off the top).
8. Seal the foil packets tightly so no steam escapes.
9. Place the foil-wrapped ribs on a baking sheet. Bake for 2.5 to 3 hours. You'll know they are ready when the meat has pulled back from the ends of the bones by about half an inch.
10. Remove the ribs from the oven and carefully open the foil.
11. Brush a thick layer of Finishing Glaze over the top of the ribs.
12. Turn your oven to Broil.
13. Place them back in the oven, uncovered, for 3-5 minutes until the sauce is bubbling and starting to caramelize into a sticky glaze. Watch them closely so they don't burn!
